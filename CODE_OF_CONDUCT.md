# LingxY Code of Conduct

LingxY is a desktop AI productivity project. We want issue threads, pull
requests, design discussions, and support channels to stay practical,
respectful, and safe for contributors with different backgrounds and levels of
experience.

## Our Pledge

We pledge to make participation in this project a harassment-free experience
for everyone, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, sex
characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience,
education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race,
religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that helps the project:

- Use welcoming and inclusive language.
- Give technical criticism with context and evidence.
- Assume good intent when discussion is ambiguous.
- Keep disagreement focused on the code, documentation, design, or product
  trade-off.
- Accept maintainer decisions after reasonable discussion.
- Credit ideas, reports, and contributions from others.

Examples of unacceptable behavior:

- Harassment, insults, slurs, threats, or personal attacks.
- Sexualized language or imagery in project spaces.
- Publishing another person's private information without explicit permission.
- Repeatedly derailing issue or pull request threads after a maintainer asks
  the discussion to move on.
- Any conduct that would reasonably make another participant feel unsafe or
  unwelcome.

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Maintainers are responsible for clarifying standards of acceptable behavior and
may remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, issues, pull requests, and other
contributions that do not align with this Code of Conduct.

Maintainers should explain moderation decisions when doing so is safe and
useful. They may act without public explanation when privacy or safety requires
it.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in all project spaces, including GitHub issues,
pull requests, discussions, documentation, release channels, and project-related
communication with maintainers.

It also applies when a person is clearly representing the project in public
spaces.

## Enforcement

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be
reported to the maintainer. See `git log` for the current maintainer contact.

Reports should include:

- A description of what happened.
- Links, screenshots, or timestamps when available.
- Whether the behavior is ongoing.
- Any safety or privacy concerns the maintainer should know before responding.

Maintainers will review reports with care and confidentiality. People involved
in enforcement decisions should recuse themselves from incidents where they have
a conflict of interest.

## Enforcement Guidelines

Maintainers may choose one or more of these responses depending on severity,
context, and prior behavior:

- Clarification: explain why the behavior is harmful and ask for a correction.
- Warning: document the issue and set expectations for future participation.
- Temporary limitation: restrict participation in specific threads or project
  spaces for a period of time.
- Permanent removal: block or ban a participant from project spaces.

The goal is not performative punishment. The goal is to keep the project
healthy, useful, and safe for contributors and users.
